Visualization Tools

There are a number of excellent visulization tools that demonstrate food insecurity or factors related to it. Some of these are specific to Denver. Here are a few that I've found:
  • Governing.com identifies Denver neighborhoods with specific markers that have gentrified in the last two decades.
  • The Statistical Atlas visualizes demographic, income, and education data for the United States down to the block groups of all 50 states.
  • The USDA Food Access Research Atlas visualizes which neighborhoods in the U.S. full under the designations of low income or low access.

Data

Here is data that was used for the visualizations on this site, and which others may find useful:
  • American Fact Finder has American Community Survey and Census data. This includes demographic and income data. It is worth noting that the ACS is not as reliable as the Census and may only have tract data where the ACS has block groups.
  • The Denver Open Data Catalog has food store data specifying supermarket and convenience store locations.
  • Denver Regional Council of Governments visualizes which neighborhoods in the U.S. full under the designations of low income or low access.
